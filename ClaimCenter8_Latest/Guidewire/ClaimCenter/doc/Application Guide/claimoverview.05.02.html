Claim Contents

Every claim is a collection of the following screens and sections of screens:
•
Summary – Lists the most salient information about the claim. See “Claim Summary Screens”.
•
Workplan – Shows initial activities and grows to include all activities created for the claim. See “Workplan Screen”.
•
Loss Details – A description of the types of losses, including vehicles, properties, injuries, and the causes of the losses. These screens also include claim associations, damage assessments, subrogation, catastrophes, and fraud detection information. See “Loss Details Screens”.
•
Exposures – Screens correlating policy coverages with claimants. In a workers’ compensation claim, the exposure screens are specific to this type of claim, like Medical Details, Time Loss, and so on. See “Exposures Screen”.
•
Reinsurance – If there is reinsurance for the policy, these screens show a summary of financial records for reinsurance. See “Reinsurance Screen”.
•
Parties Involved – All people, companies, users, vendors, legal venues and so on involved with the claim.
•
All information related to the Policy associated with the claim. This includes general information such as the policy number, policy type, and insured parties as well as information on associated endorsements and aggregate limits. See “Parties Involved Screens”.
•
Financials – An auditable record that includes checks, transactions, reserves, payments, recoveries, and recovery reserves. See “Financials Screens”.
•
Notes – All notes entered for the claim. See “Notes Screen”.
•
Documents – All documents that have been added to the claim. See “Documents Screen”.
•
Plan of Action – Plans for evaluations and negotiations, useful for settling complex claims without resorting to legal action. See “Plan of Action Screens”.
•
Services – Includes information on all service requests associated with the claim and communicated to vendors. See “Services Screen”.
•
Litigation – A list of legal matters and pending litigation related to the claim. See “Litigation Menu Link”.
•
History – A record of all claim events. See “History Screen”.
•
FNOL Snapshot – Saved First Notice of Loss (FNOL) data that encapsulates the initial data entered for the claim. See “FNOL Snapshot Screens”.
•
Calendar – Current and upcoming events and activities. See “Calendar Screens”
Clicking the Claim tab takes you to the Summary screen, accessible from the sidebar by navigating to Summary → Overview.

Other Aspects of Claims

ClaimCenter uses the following associated features to enable you to create and use claims:
•
ClaimCenter tracks its users, how they work together in groups and queues, and how they receive work. This tracking is useful because a claim is seldom handled by only one person. See “Users, Groups, and Regions”.
•
ClaimCenter assigns work by creating owners for claims, exposures, and other parts of a claim. It can use attributes, such a location, proximity information, and user characteristics, to make these assignments. See “Work Assignment”.
•
The New Claim wizard facilitates the collection of all information when a claim is first reported. See “Claim Creation”.
•
ClaimCenter creates descriptions of each event concerned with the claim, and keeps them in separate Incidents. An incident can be a general description of the loss, or center around each individual auto, piece of property, or injured individual.
•
Claims make use of complex financial features, such as multiple currencies and bulk invoices. See “Multiple Currencies” and “Bulk Invoices”.
•
Create, track, and manage requests for claim services to be provided by vendors. See “Services”.
•
Rate and select vendors based on their ratings by using service provider performance reviews. See “Service Provider Performance Reviews”.
•
Use the archiving feature to reduce the size of the active claims database and make it more manageable. See “Archiving”.
•
Work with the security features for contacts and many aspects of claim information. See in “Security: Roles, Permissions, and Access Controls”.
•
Use business rules and workflows to define your own business model. See “Introduction to Business Rules” in the Rules Guide.
•
View statistics for each user, including how many claims and activities have been recently opened and closed. Supervisors can see these statistics for their teams as well. See “Dashboard”.
•
With administrative permissions, find users, edit permissions, set claim metrics, manage catastrophes, and perform other administrative functions. See “Administering ClaimCenter”.
•
Work with users, parties involved in claims, companies and vendors, and legal venues. ClaimCenter can integrate with ContactManager to provide full, centralized contact management for vendors and claim contacts. See the “Administering ClaimCenter”.
