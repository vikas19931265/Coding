Updating Your OSGi Plugin Project after Product Location Changes

After you initially configure a project within IntelliJ IDEA with OSGi Editor, the project includes a build.properties file that references the Guidewire product directory on your local disk.
If you move your product directory, you must update your build.properties with the new product location on your local disk. There is no one place within IntelliJ IDEA with OSGi Editor to update the path.
Similarly, if you upgrade your Guidewire product to a new version with a different install path, you must update the OSGi project.
To update your OSGi project after Product Location Changes
1.
If you need to move your OSGi project on disk, quit IntelliJ IDEA with OSGi Editor and move the files on disk.
2.
Launch IntelliJ IDEA with OSGi Editor from your new Guidewire product location. See “Launch IntelliJ IDEA with OSGi Editor”.
3.
In IntelliJ IDEA with OSGi Editor, open your OSGi plugin project.
4.
In IntelliJ IDEA with OSGi Editor, update the module dependency for your OSGi module:
a.
Open the Project Structure window.
b.
Click the Modules item in the left navigation.
c.
In the list of modules to the right, click the name of your module.
d.
Click the Dependencies tab.
e.
In the list of dependencies, find and click the dependency row that ends with the text \java-api\lib.
f.
Find and click the red minus sign immediately to the right of that dependency pane. Be careful not to click the red minus sign above the modules list by accident.
g.
Above the red minus sign you clicked, click the green plus sign to add a dependency.
h.
From the picker that appears, select Jars or directories.
i.
Navigate to the ClaimCenter/java-api/lib folder in your new product location. Select the folder and click OK.
5.
In IntelliJ IDEA with OSGi Editor, edit the build.properties file at the root of the module. Change the -gw-dist-directory property to the new product location on disk. For example, that line might show:
-gw-dist-directory=C:\guidewire\ClaimCenter801
6.
Test your new configuration. See “Compiling and Installing Your OSGi Plugin as an OSGi Bundle”.
