Changes to Line of Business Typecodes

Guidewire has changed the line of business typecodes in ClaimCenter 7.0 to match those used by PolicyCenter. Guidewire updated the typecodes in ClaimCenter to simplify integration between ClaimCenter and PolicyCenter.
The upgrade does not convert any data from the old codes to the new codes. If your ClaimCenter implementation is already in production with existing typecodes, you can ignore the new typelists during the upgrade.
In ClaimCenter 7.0:
•
There are no longer two separate Workers’ Compensation coverage types for medical and indemnity. ClaimCenter 7.0 has one Statutory Workers’ Comp coverage type with coverage subtypes for the medical and indemnity exposure types.
•
For Personal Auto and Commercial Auto policy types, ClaimCenter 7.0 has one liability coverage type for bodily injury and property damage with different subtypes for different exposures. Prior versions of ClaimCenter included two separate coverage types for bodily injury and property damage.
•
ClaimCenter 7.0 has different subtypes of a single Comprehensive General Liability coverage instead of several different General Liability coverages for premise, product and other things.
•
There are no liability coverages on a Commercial Property policy.
•
ClaimCenter still has the same coverages for some lines of business that are not implemented in PolicyCenter.
•
There is no longer a single Auto line of business. Now you must choose Commercial Auto Line or Personal Auto Line. You can also no longer use the same coverage to mean collision coverage on either type of policy. Each policy type has its own set of coverages. In PolicyCenter each line of business has its own set of coverage codes. The same line of business can be included on multiple policy types, such as a monoline Commercial Property policy or a package policy with Commercial Property and General Liability.
•
ClaimCenter 7.0 still includes a General Liability line of business, now called General Liability Line. However this line is no longer a catch-all category for every liability policy. A new LOBCode, Other Liability, serves that purpose for the existing collection of extra policy types, such as Professional liability, Directors and officers, and so forth.
•

•
Commercial Package Policy (CPP) contains the lines Commercial Property, General Liability, Inland Marine, and often more. In ClaimCenter 7.0, that means that CPP is a PolicyType that is included under all of these lines of business. The CPP policy type has all the same coverages as the Commercial Property, General Liability, and Inland Marine policy types.
•
If there was only one coverage subtype for a coverage, Guidewire created a generic subtype with the same name and code as the parent coverage.
•
ClaimCenter 7.0 includes a new typelist called SourceSystem. This typelist indicates the source system for a typecode. Many typecodes now have an extra category element with SourceSystem, so the product model generator knows which typecodes originated in PolicyCenter and which typecodes originated in other sources.
•
Prior to ClaimCenter 7.0, LOBCode and LossType codes were often the same. In ClaimCenter 7.0 the LOBCode and LossType codes are usually different.
